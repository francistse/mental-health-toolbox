## 1. Not Professional Medical Advice

This toolbox provides AI-powered crisis detection and emotional support features. It is designed as a **supplementary tool** for mental health awareness and **does not constitute**:

- Professional psychological counseling or therapy
- Medical diagnosis or treatment
- Crisis intervention by trained professionals
- A replacement for licensed mental health services

**Always seek the advice of qualified mental health professionals** for any mental health concerns or before making any decisions regarding treatment.

## 2. Limitations and Risks

### 2.1 Detection Accuracy

The crisis detection system uses automated algorithms to identify potential mental health crisis indicators. However:

- **False Negatives:** The system may fail to detect genuine crisis situations, potentially missing users who need help
- **False Positives:** The system may incorrectly identify non-crisis situations as crises, causing unnecessary alarm
- **Linguistic Limitations:** Detection accuracy varies across languages, cultural contexts, and communication styles
- **Context Understanding:** AI may misinterpret sarcasm, humor, creative writing, or academic discussion as personal distress

### 2.2 Technical Risks

The software and skills implemented may malfunction unintentionally:

- **Code Errors:** Bugs, logic errors, or edge cases may cause incorrect risk assessments or inappropriate responses
- **Model Limitations:** AI language models have inherent limitations in understanding nuanced emotional states
- **Configuration Issues:** Incorrect setup or integration may impair functionality
- **Version Compatibility:** Updates to AI agents or dependencies may break existing functionality
- **Network/Infrastructure:** Service disruptions may prevent access to crisis resources

### 2.3 Response Limitations

- Automated responses may be inappropriate, insensitive, or harmful in certain situations
- The praise group feature may trivialize serious concerns or provide inadequate support
- Crisis resource recommendations may be outdated or unavailable in your region

## 6. Emergency Situations

**If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or experiencing a mental health crisis:**

- **Call your local emergency number** (e.g., 911 in US/Canada, 999 in UK, 112 in EU)
- **Contact a crisis hotline** (see resources below)
- **Go to the nearest emergency room**
- **Reach out to a trusted mental health professional**
